Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 7, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how CatPlay2 ("the App", "we", "us", or "our") handles information when you use the App. CatPlay2 is a mobile game designed for cats and their owners.

1. Information We Collect

CatPlay2 does not require account registration and does not ask users to provide names, email addresses, phone numbers, contacts, photos, videos, audio recordings, messages, financial information, or health information.

However, the App uses third-party services that may automatically collect certain information for advertising, analytics, crash reporting, security, and service operation purposes. This may include:

  • Device or other identifiers, such as advertising identifiers or app instance identifiers
  • Approximate location inferred from IP address
  • App interactions, such as screen views, ad impressions, ad clicks, and rewarded ad interactions
  • Crash logs, diagnostics, and app performance data
  • Device information, such as device model, operating system version, language, and network state

2. How We Use Information

Information collected through the App or third-party services may be used for:

  • Displaying banner ads and rewarded ads
  • Allowing users to unlock premium targets through rewarded ads
  • Understanding general app usage and improving the App
  • Detecting, diagnosing, and fixing crashes or technical issues
  • Preventing fraud, abuse, and security issues
  • Complying with legal and platform requirements

3. Third-Party Services

CatPlay2 uses third-party services provided by Google. These services may collect and process data according to their own privacy policies.

  • Google AdMob: Used to display banner ads and rewarded ads. AdMob may collect information such as device identifiers, ad interactions, approximate location, and diagnostic data for advertising, fraud prevention, analytics, and service operation.
  • Firebase Analytics: Used to understand general app usage and improve the App.
  • Firebase Crashlytics: Used to collect crash reports and diagnostic information to improve app stability.

You can learn more about how Google handles data here: https://policies.google.com/privacy

You can also learn more about Google's advertising technologies here: https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads

4. Advertising

CatPlay2 displays advertisements through Google AdMob. The App may show banner advertisements and rewarded advertisements. Rewarded advertisements may be used to unlock premium targets in the App.

Advertising partners may use device identifiers, advertising identifiers, app activity, approximate location, and other technical information to provide, measure, and improve advertisements, detect fraud, and comply with legal requirements.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal information. Data may be shared with third-party service providers, such as Google AdMob and Firebase, for advertising, analytics, crash reporting, fraud prevention, security, and service operation.

6. Data Retention

CatPlay2 does not maintain user accounts or user-submitted personal profiles. Data collected by third-party services is retained according to the retention policies of those providers. Crash reports, analytics data, and advertising data may be retained by Google for the periods described in Google's own policies and service settings.

7. Data Security

We rely on third-party services that use security measures designed to protect data in transit and during processing. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Children's Privacy

CatPlay2 is designed as a game for cats and their owners. The App does not knowingly request personal information from children. If you believe that a child has provided personal information through the App, please contact us so that we can take appropriate action.

9. User Choices

Users may be able to limit ad personalization through their device settings or Google account settings. Availability of these options may vary depending on device, operating system, region, and Google services.

Android users may review or reset advertising ID settings from their device settings where available.
